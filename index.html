Dental Implants

1. Definition:

Dental implants involve the replacement of missing teeth with artificial ones. This can be done through various methods such as bridges and implants.

2. Types of Implants:

Bridges: Bridges are installed by attaching them to the adjacent teeth of the missing tooth area. Implants: Implants involve surgically placing fixtures into the jawbone and then attaching artificial teeth to these fixtures.

3. Benefits of Dental Implants:

Restoration of lost tooth functions. Improvement of facial appearance and smile. Enhancement of speech and chewing ability. Prevention of changes in jaw structure and bone loss.

4. Procedure:

Diagnosis and treatment planning. Preparation of the mouth and teeth. Installation of artificial teeth. Adjustment of teeth for consistency and comfort.

Dentures:

1. Definition:

Dentures are artificial structures used to replace all teeth in the upper or lower jaw, or both.

2. Types of Dentures:

Full Dentures: Used to replace all teeth in the upper or lower jaw or both. Partial Dentures: Used to replace some teeth when there are natural teeth remaining.

3. Benefits of Dentures:

Restoration of tooth functions and chewing. Improvement of external appearance and smile. Prevention of changes in jaw structure. Support for speech and speaking.

4. Procedure:

Diagnosis of the condition and treatment planning. Preparation of the mouth and teeth (for full dentures). Design and manufacture of dentures. Adjustment of dentures for comfort and consistency.

Oral Health Care:

Proper Cleaning: Dentures and implants require daily cleaning. Regular Dental Visits: Regular check-ups with a dentist are essential for monitoring the condition of dentures and implants and making adjustments if necessary.
